Overview

Boston has the cheapest STR registration in this batch at $25/year. The city distinguishes between owner-occupied units (unlimited, no caps) and investor units (limited availability). The 12.5% combined tax rate is moderate for a Northeast city. Boston's market is driven by universities (50+ colleges), medical tourism, and business travel — creating year-round demand with less seasonal variance than leisure markets. The $25/year fee is almost symbolic, making Boston one of the lowest-barrier STR cities for owner-occupants.

License Application — Step by Step

  1. Determine unit type: owner-occupied vs investor
  2. Register STR with City of Boston ($25/year)
  3. Register for room occupancy excise tax
  4. Display registration number on listings
  5. Renew annually

Taxes

6.5% Massachusetts Room Occupancy Excise + 6% Boston Convention Center Financing Fee = 12.5%. Platforms collect.

Key Operating Rules

  • Owner-occupied units: no cap, no limit
  • Investor units: limited availability
  • 12.5% combined tax (platforms collect)
  • $1M liability insurance recommended
  • Noise ordinances enforced
  • Building must meet safety codes

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • $300/day for unregistered listing
