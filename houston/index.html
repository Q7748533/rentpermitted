Overview

Houston has the cheapest STR registration in this batch at $90/year, with no primary residence requirement, no density caps, and no night limits. Unlike Dallas, platforms DO collect hotel occupancy tax in Houston — removing the self-remittance burden. Houston is the fourth-largest US city with massive business travel (energy, medical, aerospace) and convention demand. The regulatory environment is among the most permissive in the country. The trade-off: Houston is a sprawled, car-dependent city where location near business centers matters enormously for occupancy.

License Application — Step by Step

  1. Register STR with City of Houston ($90/year)
  2. Hotels tax: platforms collect and remit automatically
  3. Display registration number on listings
  4. Renew annually

Taxes

7% City of Houston + 6% Texas state HOT = 13%. Platforms collect and remit in Houston.

Key Operating Rules

  • No primary residence requirement — pure investment allowed
  • No density cap
  • No night limit
  • HOT collected by platforms (unlike Dallas)
  • Noise and nuisance ordinances enforced

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • $100–$500/day for operating without certificate
  • Platforms must remove non-compliant listings within 10 days of city notice
  • Certificate revocation for non-compliance or excessive nuisance

Is Houston STR-Friendly?

Houston is the easiest major US city to run an STR. $90/year registration with zero regulatory barriers. No primary residence, no caps, no night limits, platform-collected taxes. The ADR is lower than destination markets, but the compliance simplicity and scalability are unmatched. For investors who want regulatory certainty above all else, Houston is the answer.
