Regulatory Overview

Arizona is the strongest investor market in our coverage, anchored by SB1350 — a 2016 law that prohibits cities from banning short-term rentals outright. Cities may require licenses, collect taxes, and enforce nuisance ordinances, but cannot use zoning to restrict STRs. Both Scottsdale and Phoenix operate under this framework with straightforward annual registration ($250/year) and no primary residence requirements. Arizona's state-level preemption provides regulatory certainty that few other states offer, making it one of the most predictable markets for STR investment.
