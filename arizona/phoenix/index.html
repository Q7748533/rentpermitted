Overview

Phoenix benefits from the same Arizona SB1350 state preemption as Scottsdale: cities cannot ban STRs, only regulate for health and safety. This makes Phoenix fundamentally accessible to investors. The $250/year license is affordable, and there's no primary residence requirement or night cap. The market is larger than Scottsdale with more diverse price points : from budget to luxury. Summer occupancy drops in desert heat, but winter/spring demand from snowbirds, spring training, and events is strong. Phoenix is one of the few major US cities where non-owner-occupied STR investment is straightforward and legal.

Regulation Digest

Phoenix allows short-term rentals with registration. The process is straightforward compared to more restrictive cities. The license costs $250/year. A combined occupancy tax of ~12.57% combined transient lodging tax (state + county + city) applies to all bookings under 30 nights. No night limit applies: but zoning and density restrictions may still apply.

License Application: Step by Step

  1. Apply for Phoenix STR License ($250/year)
  2. Register for AZ TPT License (state)
  3. Provide emergency contact information
  4. Maintain liability insurance
  5. Display license number on all listings
  6. Renew annually

Key Operating Rules

  • Arizona SB1350: city cannot ban STRs
  • No primary residence requirement
  • No annual night limit
  • Emergency contact must be available 24/7
  • Noise and nuisance ordinances enforced
  • HOA rules may be more restrictive

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • 1st violation: min $500 or 1 night's rental fee (whichever greater)
  • 2nd violation: min $1,000 or 2 nights' rental fee
  • 3rd violation: min $3,500 or 3 nights' rental fee
  • 3 violations in 12 months = 12-month permit suspension
  • Source: phoenix.gov STR Registry page

Enforcement Reality

Same SB1350 framework as Scottsdale: the city cannot ban STRs but enforces nuisance, noise, and safety violations. Phoenix requires annual registration and neighbor notification. HB 2429 (pending 2026) would allow cities to set occupancy standards : two adults per sleeping area : and extend permit violation records to 24 months. Enforcement is complaint-driven rather than proactive, but repeat violators face escalating penalties.

Is Phoenix STR-Friendly?

Phoenix is a top-tier STR investment market. Arizona's SB1350 provides regulatory certainty that most other states lack. No primary residence, no night cap, cheap license, massive snowbird/event demand. The trade-off is summer heat: occupancy and ADR drop significantly June-September. But the annual economics still work, and the regulatory environment means you can plan with confidence. Along with Scottsdale, Phoenix is the most investor-friendly major city in this 25-city batch.

Bottom line: Phoenix is a top-tier STR investment market.
