Overview

Asheville requires homestay permits — owners must live on-site during all rentals and can only rent up to 2 bedrooms. Whole-home (non-owner-occupied) STRs are effectively banned in most residential zones. This makes Asheville a room-rental market for homeowners, not an investment market. The ~16.75% combined tax rate is among the highest in this batch, reflecting the city's reliance on tourism revenue. Asheville's tourism (Biltmore, Blue Ridge, craft beer) creates strong demand, but the regulatory model funnels most of it to hotels and B&Bs.

License Application — Step by Step

  1. Verify primary residence and homestay eligibility
  2. Apply for Homestay Permit ($200/year)
  3. Register for occupancy tax collection
  4. Limit rentals to 2 bedrooms maximum
  5. Be present during all guest stays
  6. Renew annually

Taxes

4.75% NC state sales + 6% Buncombe County occupancy + 6% City of Asheville occupancy = ~16.75% (per L6 sources). Verify at ashevillenc.gov Fees and Charges Manual.

Is Asheville STR-Friendly?

Asheville is a homeowner's side-income market, not an investment destination. Strong tourism demand exists but the homestay-only model caps revenue at ~$25K/year (2 bedrooms max). The ~16.75% tax rate is punishing. For Asheville homeowners with spare rooms: decent side income. For investors: no path.
