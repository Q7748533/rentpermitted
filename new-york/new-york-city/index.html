Overview

NYC effectively banned most STRs with Local Law 18 (2023). Hosts must register with OSE, be present during stays, limit guests to 2. Entire-apartment rentals under 30 days are prohibited in most multi-dwelling buildings. Airbnb removed ~80% of NYC listings after enforcement. This is a hosted room-share market for owner-occupants only.

Regulation Digest

Short-term rentals under 30 days are effectively banned in New York City. Enforcement is active and penalties are severe. The license costs $145/2 years. A combined occupancy tax of ~14.75% (5.875% NYC hotel + 8.875% state/city sales) applies to all bookings under 30 nights. No night limit applies: but zoning and density restrictions may still apply.
