Regulatory Overview

Louisiana has no state-level STR preemption, leaving regulation to cities and parishes. New Orleans operates one of the country's most complex STR systems: a permit lottery with caps on non-owner-occupied Commercial STR (CSTR) permits, plus platform enforcement scheduled for Q3 2026. The French Quarter is entirely off-limits for whole-home STRs. CSTR permits cost $1,000/year with a $50 application fee; owner-occupied Residential STR (NSTR) permits are $500. Event-driven tourism : Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, Essence Fest, Sugar Bowl : creates extraordinary peak pricing demand that no other US city matches for concentrated calendar events. But the lottery + cap + French Quarter ban makes this a demand-rich, regulation-hostile market for new investors. Established permit holders benefit from the scarcity; newcomers face steep odds.
