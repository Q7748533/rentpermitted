Overview

Charleston uses a two-tier permit system: Class 1 for occasional STR use (24-72 nights/year, $250 permit) and Class 2 for frequent STR operation (72+ nights, $1,500/year). Both require a $200 zoning review. The city's historic district regulations add complexity : some zones prohibit STRs entirely. Hosts must self-remit the ~14% combined tax (platforms don't collect in Charleston). Enforcement is serious: fines up to $1,087/day and potential criminal prosecution. Charleston's tourism (historic charm, food scene, Spoleto) drives very strong demand, but the regulatory structure adds significant cost and administrative burden for full-time operators.

License Application : Step by Step

  1. Verify zoning allows STR (historic district restrictions apply)
  2. Choose Class 1 (24-72 nights) or Class 2 (72+ nights)
  3. Submit zoning review ($200)
  4. Apply for STR permit ($250 or $1,500)
  5. Register for accommodations tax (self-remit)
  6. Display permit on listings
  7. Renew annually

Taxes

5% SC state sales + 2% state accommodations + 1% local option + 1% county transportation + 1% school district + 2% Charleston County accommodations + 2% City of Charleston = 14% total (Charleston County). Host must self-remit; platforms may NOT collect local tax. Source: charleston-sc.gov Accommodations Tax page.

Key Operating Rules

  • Class 1: 24-72 nights/year maximum
  • Class 2: 72+ nights/year, $1,500 fee
  • Historic district zoning restrictions : some zones prohibit STRs
  • ~14% tax self-remitted (platforms don't collect)
  • Strict noise and nuisance enforcement
  • Criminal prosecution possible for violations

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Civil penalty: $500-$2,000 per 7-day period of non-compliance
  • Up to $1,087/day in fines (city code)
  • 30 days jail possible
  • Criminal prosecution for continued violations

Is Charleston STR-Friendly?

Charleston is a premium STR market with premium compliance costs. $1,700 first-year permit + self-remit taxes + criminal penalties make it one of the most administratively demanding cities in this batch. But the market rewards: #1 US city ranking, premium ADRs, and recession-resistant historic tourism. For committed operators with properties in correct zones and tolerance for administrative overhead, Charleston delivers strong returns. For casual or budget operators: look elsewhere.
