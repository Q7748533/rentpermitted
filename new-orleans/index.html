Overview

New Orleans has one of the most complex STR regulatory systems: a lottery for residential permits, outright bans in the French Quarter, and caps that vary by district. Residential STRs require primary residence and are limited in number — the lottery system adds uncertainty. Commercial STRs cost $1,000/year but face their own district caps. The French Quarter ban (with narrow Bourbon Street exceptions) removes the highest-demand zone. Enforcement has been inconsistent historically but is tightening.

  5. Pay $5-$12/night occupancy fee per bedroom
  6. Display permit number on all listings
  7. Renew annually

Is New Orleans STR-Friendly?

New Orleans is a demand-rich, regulation-hostile market. The event-driven tourism (Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest) creates extraordinary peak pricing, but the lottery + cap + French Quarter ban system makes reliable access difficult. For commercial property owners outside the French Quarter: CSTR path can work with proper due diligence. For residential investors: the NSTR lottery is not a reliable bet.
