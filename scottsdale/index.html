Overview

Scottsdale benefits from Arizona's SB1350 — a state preemption law that prohibits cities from banning short-term rentals outright. This makes Scottsdale fundamentally more investor-friendly than cities in states without such protections. Scottsdale can regulate for health and safety (licensing, noise, insurance, emergency contacts) but cannot zone STRs out of existence. The result: a regulated but accessible market with strong luxury-tourism demand and some of the highest ADRs in the country. Scottsdale's winter/spring occupancy rates hit 72%+, while summer slumps to desert lows. The license is $250/year with a mandatory $500K liability insurance requirement and neighbor notification. The key compliance risk is the emergency contact rule: if your designated contact fails to respond to a complaint within 1 hour, you face fines directly.

License Application — Step by Step

  1. Apply for Scottsdale STR License online via city portal ($250/year)
  2. Register for Arizona TPT License via AZTaxes.gov (no fee)
  3. Notify adjacent property owners of STR operation
  4. Provide proof of $500,000 minimum liability insurance
  5. Designate 24/7 emergency contact who can respond within 1 hour
  6. Display license number on all platform listings
  7. Renew city license annually
  8. Remit TPT taxes to state (monthly or quarterly depending on volume)

Taxes

Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT) on stays under 30 days. State base rate + Scottsdale surcharge. Stays 30+ days exempt from city TPT as of Jan 2025. Platforms may or may not collect — verify.

Key Operating Rules

  • State preemption: Scottsdale cannot ban STRs — only regulate health, safety, and nuisance (SB1350)
  • Minimum $500,000 liability insurance required per property
  • Neighbor notification mandatory at application and upon emergency contact changes
  • 24/7 emergency contact must respond and arrive within 1 hour of complaint
  • Noise ordinance enforced — nuisance party rules with escalating civil penalties
  • Stays under 30 days: subject to TPT. Stays 30+ days: exempt from city TPT (as of Jan 2025)
  • HOA rules can be more restrictive than city — check CC&Rs

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • $500 for first violation
  • Up to $2,000 for repeated violations
  • License revocation for persistent non-compliance (property banned from STR use)
  • Owner cited if emergency contact fails to respond within 1 hour
