Overview

93|

Scottsdale benefits from Arizona's SB1350 — a state preemption law that prohibits cities from banning short-term rentals outright. This makes Scottsdale fundamentally more investor-friendly than cities in states without such protections. Scottsdale can regulate for health and safety (licensing, noise, insurance, emergency contacts) but cannot zone STRs out of existence. The result: a regulated but accessible market with strong luxury-tourism demand and some of the highest ADRs in the country. Scottsdale's winter/spring occupancy rates hit 72%+, while summer slumps to desert lows. The license is $250/year with a mandatory $500K liability insurance requirement and neighbor notification. The key compliance risk is the emergency contact rule: if your designated contact fails to respond to a complaint within 1 hour, you face fines directly.

Taxes

117|

Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT) on stays under 30 days. State base rate + Scottsdale surcharge. Stays 30+ days exempt from city TPT as of Jan 2025. Platforms may or may not collect — verify.

Is Scottsdale STR-Friendly?

198|

Scottsdale is the strongest investor market in this batch. Arizona's SB1350 state preemption removes the existential regulatory risk that plagues Nashville, Denver, and Orlando. The license is cheap, the ADR is premium, and peak-season demand is world-class. The trade-off is seasonality: summer months are slow and hot. But with $52K+ average annual revenue and a straightforward compliance path, the ROI math works. For out-of-state investors looking for a luxury STR market with regulatory certainty, Scottsdale delivers.
