Overview

Austin requires all short-term rentals — any residential property rented for fewer than 30 consecutive days — to hold a valid operating license from Austin Development Services Code Compliance. The city uses a three-tier system (Type 1, Type 2, Type 3) that determines where and how you can operate.

⚠️ Critical Deadline — July 1, 2026: Starting this date, Airbnb, Vrbo, and Booking.com must display valid Austin STR license numbers on all listings. Unlicensed properties will be removed within 10 days of a city request.

License Application — Step by Step

What You Need

  • Copy of owner's driver's license or government ID
  • Proof of tenancy (if the applicant is a tenant)
  • Notarized Agent Authorization Form (if applicant is not the property owner)
  • Certificate of Occupancy & proof of insurance no longer required (removed Oct 2025)

How to Submit

  1. Go to Austin Finance Online (AFO)
  2. Upload required documents
  3. Pay fee via credit card or e-check
  4. Email a copy to STRLicensing@austintexas.gov

Taxes — Hotel Occupancy Tax (HOT)

Austin charges a combined 17% Hotel Occupancy Tax:

  • City of Austin: 11%
  • State of Texas: 6%

Platforms like Airbnb and Vrbo must collect and remit HOT on your behalf. You must file a quarterly report with the City — including a zero report if you had no rentals.

HOT questions: hotels@austintexas.gov | 512-974-2590 (press 1)

Key Operating Rules

Local Contact Requirement

If you don't live in the Austin Metro Area (Travis, Williamson, Hays, Bastrop, or Caldwell County), designate a local contact who can respond to emergencies within 2 hours, 24/7.

Guest Noise Restrictions

  • No sound equipment >75 decibels at property line (10 a.m.–10 p.m.)
  • No sound equipment audible beyond property line (10 p.m.–10 a.m.)
  • No noise or music audible to adjacent residences (10:30 p.m.–7 a.m.)

Neighbor Notification

The City mails your contact information to all properties within 100 feet at every license issuance and renewal.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Operating without a license: fines + listing removal starting July 1, 2026
  • Advertising an unlicensed STR: prohibited under City Ordinance §4-23-34
  • Repeated noise or nuisance violations: license revocation

Is Austin STR-Friendly?

Austin is moderately restrictive. Type 1 (owner-occupied) is manageable. Type 2 non-owner-occupied properties face the 1,000-foot spacing cap — making it increasingly difficult for investors. The July 2026 enforcement will remove many illegal listings.
